Heritable genome editing in C. elegans via a crisPr-cas9 system

In this article the researcher performed CRISPR Cas9 gene editing in the Caenorhabditis elegans germ line using single-guide RNAs and successfully established heritable genetic alterations in C. elegans. A step by step procedure was provided on how to identify U6 snRNA pol III promoter and target sequence for the sgRNA. It describes the complete construct of the plasmid and how Cas9, sgRNAs and mCherry marker vectors were microinjected into adult worms. The RNA was later isolated and identified by performing RT-PCR assays. The result of the disruption was detected from f1 generation. Inheritance of targeted disruptions was studied along the f2 progeny.
