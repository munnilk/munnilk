Effect of CRISPER gene drive system in the wild population

In this article the researcher produced a mathematical model to calculate efficiency of gene drive system is effcted by the drive resistant allel. Presence of drive-resistant allele reduces the efficiency of CRISPR-based gene drive systems. Mathematical tools were developed to analyse the efficiency of the gene drive system. The aim is to quantify the possibility and extent of the spread of the gene drive system. One of the crucial steps of the CRISPR-based gene drive system is the breaking of the double strand. The break in the double strand is repaired by either of the two way, homology directed repair or non-homologous repair pathway. When the nonhomologous pathway is the used for the repairing of the broken DNA, there is a possibility of creating mutation due to the mechanism.
